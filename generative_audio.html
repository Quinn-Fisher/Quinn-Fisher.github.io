Generated Audio

This is really a work in progress and more exploratory than my other work. I like to mess around with audio and music, so I figured this would be a fun investigation. The goal of this section is to investigate two things:

  1. How can I generate interesting music using diffusion networks
  2. How can I manipulate "encodings" to get interesting sounding generated audio that shares characteristics of the source audio. In other words, what is an effective way to traverse the latent space.

Once I implemented the great work done by Robert Smith, my first thought was to mess around with the encodings (inupts to the network) using convex combinations. This is mostly due to my research with mixup showing that when you use convex combinations inputs in deep networks, interesting and somewhat undexpected behaviours occur. Below is an example of me first generating two encodings, \(E_1\) and \(E_1\). I then take convex combinations of the encodings, \( \lambda E_1 + (1 - \lambda)E_2 \) and vary \(\lambda\) from 0 to 1. I use each of these convex combinations as inputs for the network and stitch together the generated audio, yielding the "music" below.

Sounds great, right? Ok... maybe in an avant-garde way, but I think it sounds pretty cool. This method for navigating the latent space isnt very sophisticated, so maybe we should try something more motivated.
